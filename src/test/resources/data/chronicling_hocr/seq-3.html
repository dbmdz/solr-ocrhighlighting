HODGES WON’T HI N Has Neither Time, Money Nor Inclina­ tion to Make Unco. Little Rock, Jan. 4.—(Special) Farle W. Hodges will not be a can­ didate for governor at the 11*20 pri­ maries Jnst before his departure for the west last night, he said that he had neither time, money nor inclination to make the race, and that, notwith­ standing recent rumors that he might run, he had not had any serious in­ tention of doing so. He is managing the campaign in the far west for Jewish Relief, and since July 13 the states under his manage­ ment have raised in actual cash $2,­ 125,000, which has been deposited in New York banks. For several months his headquart­ ers will be at Los Angeles, Calif., the quota for that state being $1,000,000. After completing the work in Califor­ nia, ho will go to Oregon to raise $525,000, and to Washington to raise $535,000. He expects to be gone from Arkansas at least four months. Mr. Hodges says that he is too busy now to think of mixing up in politics again.
